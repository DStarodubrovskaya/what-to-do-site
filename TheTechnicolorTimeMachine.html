Books: The Technicolor Time Machine

January 31, 2024

The Technicolor Time Machine

Author: Harry Harrison
Genre: Science fiction

My Opinion:
"The film studio is facing a crisis that threatens bankruptcy. They decide to travel back in time and make a film about Vikings in real locations. With real Vikings, of course. A very interesting surreal. Be sure to read and re-read!"

The Technicolor Time Machine is a 1967 science fiction novel by American writer Harry Harrison. It is a time travel story with comedic elements, which satirizes Hollywood. The story first appeared in Analog Science Fiction and Fact magazine, where it was serialized in three parts in the March–May 1967 issues, under the title "The Time-Machined Saga."

Plot

Barney Hendrickson, a struggling filmmaker, faces the collapse of Climactic Studios due to the mismanagement of its owner, L. M. Greenspan. In a last-ditch effort to save the studio, Hendrickson enlists the help of Professor Hewitt and his time machine. They plan to make a historical movie in the past, recruiting a Viking named Ottar as a guide. As they encounter challenges in both the past and present, including a broken time machine and impending bankruptcy, Hendrickson must find a way to complete the film. With the unexpected arrival of his future self, Hendrickson learns how to overcome their obstacles and finish the movie in time, despite a few comical mishaps along the way.
