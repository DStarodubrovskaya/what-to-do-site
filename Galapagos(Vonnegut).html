Books: Galápagos

February 26, 2024

Galápagos

Author: Kurt Vonnegut
Genre: Novel, Science fiction

My Opinion:
"People have played it out. The apocalypse happened. Of all humanity, only 10 unfortunates remained.
In this book you will learn how they got to the island, and what humanity has evolved into over a million years."

Galápagos (1985) is the eleventh novel published by American author Kurt Vonnegut. Set in the Galápagos Islands after a global financial disaster, the novel questions the merit of the human brain from an evolutionary perspective. The title is both a reference to the islands on which part of the story plays out, and a tribute to Charles Darwin, on whose theory Vonnegut relies to reach his own conclusions. It was published by Delacorte Press.

Plot

Galápagos is a novel by Kurt Vonnegut set in the aftermath of a global financial disaster on the Galápagos Islands. Following a crisis that renders humanity infertile, a group of survivors, including Vietnam War veteran Leon Trotsky Trout, find themselves marooned on Santa Rosalia. Over a million years, their descendants evolve into furry, aquatic mammals resembling sea lions. The story explores themes of human evolution, the consequences of the oversized human brain, and the enduring spirit of hope.
